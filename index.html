Kurdistan, Arabic Kurdistān, Persian Kordestān, broadly defined geographic region traditionally inhabited mainly by Kurds. It consists of an extensive plateau and mountain area, spread over large parts of what are now eastern Turkey, northern Iraq, and western Iran and smaller parts of northern Syria and Armenia. Two of these countries officially recognize internal entities by this name: Iran’s northwestern province of Kordestān and Iraq’s Kurdish autonomous region. The Kurdistan (“Land of the Kurds”) designation refers to an area of Kurdish settlement that roughly includes the mountain systems of the Zagros and the eastern extension of the Taurus. Since ancient times the area has been the home of the Kurds, a people whose ethnic origins are uncertain. For 600 years after the Arab conquest and their conversion to Islam, the Kurds played a recognizable and considerable part in the troubled history of western Asia—but as tribes, individuals, or turbulent groups rather than as a people.

Erbil Citadel

The Erbil Citadel, which is also called Qelat, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2014. It’s not only a famous site in Erbil but one of the major landmarks in Iraq.

Rwanduz

The Canyon of Rawanduz Valley is in my opinion the most beautiful place in Iraqi Kurdistan. There are several viewpoints from which you can see the incredible beauty of Rawanduz. If you have more time than visiting the viewpoints, you can hike in the Valley of Rawanduz

Bekhal waterfall

About 7 km from the Rawanduz Viewpoint, you get to the Bekhal Waterfalls. This place is a super touristic place full of waterfalls, cafés and restaurants.

Geli Ali Beg

The Geli Ali Beg Waterfall is also a very touristic place.the water is everywhere and you can walk barefoot in the cold water. Prepare flip-flops and a towel for this lovely adventure.

Korek Mountain

Snow covered mountains are gorgeous! The Villas are very cozy and warm. Nice to be there when it is snowing in the winters.

Zorgvan Valley

Getting to Zoragvan and Barzan was one of craziest adventures when traveling in Iraqi Kurdistan.

Ahmad Awa Waterfall

A beautiful waterfall to discover in Kurdistan. To get there you need to pass small villages which is very nice and you get to see a wonderful view over the mountains and the valleys.

Piramagrun Mountain

Piramagrun mountain have very beautiful places to visit as it has several valleys from many sides of it. One valley from Swse village leads to a very beautiful nature valley with an interesting cave, Gechkar cave, where you need a guide with you to find it .

Darbandikhan Lake

Beautiful nature around this lake and it’s perfect to visit this area during spring and autumn. Good for family travels. This area is famous for its variety of wildlife.

Qashqoli River

A river flowing from Dukan lake with lots of touristic houses or villas to stay overnight. Nice to hike to some mountains. Very busy during weekends during spring time. Weather is best during spring and autumn. It can be hot in summer.

Gole

This beautiful place is indeed the hidden jewelry of Penjwin town, it’s around 25 minutes away by driving from Penjwin town or 2 hours away by driving from Suly city.

Hawraman

Highly recommend to visit if you are by any chance in Suly city. You can visit it in any season. Winter is cold and lots of snowing, spring is amazingly green and autumn is typical yellowish and beautiful with walnut and pomegranate collection time.

Halamata cave

It takes about 30 minutes to climb down from the parking on top of the mountain. you probably won't find it without a local guide as the place is perfectly hidden in the middle of nowhere.

Zawa Mountain

Zawa Mountain Is Behind Duhok And It Has Fantastic View And In Zawa Has A Small Cafe And Has Restaurant

Azadi Park

there is a lot of nice amusement games in it, like twist tower, control plane flying chair mini pendulum and others, it is a nice place to visit.

Good place for shopping and going out to there with friends and family for eating and shopping

Newroz

Newroz is the Kurdish celebration of the Persian new year holiday “Nowruz.” Kurdish Newroz coincides with the Spring Equinox, and is a festival celebrating the beginning of spring. Over the years, Newroz has come to represent new beginnings, as well as an opportunity to support the Kurdish cause. For these reasons, Newroz is considered to be the most important festival in Kurdish culture. Typically the festival is celebrated in the days running up to the Spring Equinox, and this year will be celebrated from March 21th to April 1st.

Eid al-Fitr

first of two canonical festivals of Islam. Eid al-Fitr marks the end of Ramadan, the Muslim holy month of fasting, and is celebrated during the first three days of Shawwal, the 10th month of the Islamic calendar.

Eid al-adha

The day of Eid-ul-Adha falls on the tenth day in the final (twelfth) month of the Islamic Lunar Calendar; Dhu-al-Hijjah. The day that celebrations fall on is dependent on a legitimate sighting of the moon, following the completion of the annual Holy Pilgrimage of Hajj - which is an obligation for all Muslim’s who fit specific criteria, one of the important Five Pillars of Islam.

New Year

New Year festival, any of the social, cultural, and religious observances worldwide that celebrate the beginning of the new year. Such festivals are among the oldest and the most universally observed.
